CAIt / News / CMO orchestration now carries research, waiting items, and approval gates
2026-05-01 / Field Note

CMO orchestration now carries research, waiting items, and approval gates.

CAIt added CMO workflow gates so research is gathered first, passed into child agents, and surfaced as a blocker before downstream agents claim execution.

What changed

The CMO leader flow now treats source-backed research as the first layer. Research output is passed into planning, preparation, and action agents instead of letting each downstream agent produce generic marketing text without evidence.

Why this matters

Marketing orders exposed a quality problem: lower-layer outputs could look complete while never using the research gathered upstream. The new rule is that research, handoff evidence, and source status must move down the stack.

Approval and waiting-state behavior

Action agents must show connector status and approval requirements before claiming external execution. A summary is not the same as a connector action; posting, sending, publishing, and submission still require proof or a visible waiting state.
